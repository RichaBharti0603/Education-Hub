What is the Pythagorean theorem?|The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. This can be written as: a^2 + b^2 = c^2, where c is the hypotenuse.
